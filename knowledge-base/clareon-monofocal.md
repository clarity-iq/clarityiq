# Clareon Monofocal — Patient Labeling

**1. Metadata**
- Lens: ALCON Aspheric Monofocal IOL (Clareon and AcrySof material versions)
- Manufacturer: Alcon Laboratories, Inc.
- Category: Standard monofocal IOL, with an aspheric surface enhancement (see §3)
- PMA: P190018 (base approval, decided 2020-01-07)
- Source: FDA Patient Information Brochure "ALCON Toric Intraocular Lenses" (covers Toric and Aspheric Monofocal comparatively), `accessdata.fda.gov/cdrh_docs/pdf19/P190018D.pdf`, document TOR11343PE

**2. Indications**
Capsular bag implantation to replace the natural lens after cataract removal, in adults. Designed to restore distance vision. Does not correct pre-existing corneal astigmatism. Designed to benefit a person with an average corneal shape.

**3. How it works**
A monofocal (single fixed focal point) lens. The aspheric surface is designed to enhance distance vision specifically in low-light conditions, for patients wearing full correction glasses — this benefit requires wearing glasses to correct any residual refractive error, it isn't glasses-free. Does not provide near vision; patients will need reading glasses. Does not correct astigmatism — patients with pre-existing corneal astigmatism will likely still need glasses for distance vision too.

**4. Expected visual outcomes**
From the FDA clinical study (non-toric/monofocal arm, n=210, uncorrected distance vision at 6 months post-op):
- 20/40 or better without glasses: 77.1%
- 20/20 or better without glasses: 19.0%

(Comparator data from the same study: toric arm achieved 93.8% at 20/40-or-better and 38.4% at 20/20-or-better — see the Clareon Toric document. Far-vision-without-glasses results are generally better for patients with low pre-existing astigmatism than high, regardless of lens choice.)

**5. Known side effects / visual disturbances** *(flagged for Martin's review — not auto-approved)*
General cataract-surgery risks apply: reactions to medicines, redness, scratchiness, light sensitivity, infection, bleeding, inflammation, tissue damage/swelling, increased eye pressure. Since this lens doesn't correct astigmatism, patients with meaningful pre-existing corneal astigmatism may experience visual distortions (objects appearing tilted, floors appearing curved) if left uncorrected by glasses. No multifocal-type disturbances (halos/glare/starbursts) are associated with this design.

**6. Contraindications / poor-candidate factors**
Safety and effectiveness not established in patients with pre-existing eye conditions or surgical complications such as glaucoma or diabetic retinopathy. Surgical outcome depends on the health of the eye before surgery. Patients with significant pre-existing corneal astigmatism will not have it corrected by this lens — a toric option should be discussed with the surgeon instead.

**7. Source citation**
PMA P190018 (base). Patient Information Brochure: `https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/cdrh_docs/pdf19/P190018D.pdf` (document TOR11343PE — covers ALCON Toric, Aspheric Monofocal, and Spherical Monofocal IOLs comparatively; Table 2 non-toric arm cited above).
