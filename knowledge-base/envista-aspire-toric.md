# enVista Aspire Toric — Patient Labeling

**1. Metadata**
- Lens: enVista® ASPIRE Toric IOL, Model ETA
- Manufacturer: Bausch + Lomb, Incorporated
- Category: "Enhanced monofocal" IOL (same optic technology as enVista Aspire) combined with a toric astigmatism-correcting surface
- PMA: P910056, Supplement S051 (Normal 180 Day Track, decided 2023-08-21) — same supplement that introduced non-toric enVista Aspire
- **Market status note:** Same platform family as enVista Envy and enVista Aspire — per the master list, the April 2025 TASS-related recall affected enVista products broadly; confirmed back on market. Flagged for informed patient discussion.
- **Source note:** No FDA SSED or patient brochure is publicly posted for this supplement, and **no dedicated peer-reviewed study of this specific toric-enhanced-monofocal combination was found** (a targeted PubMed search returned zero results). This is the thinnest evidence base of any lens in this knowledge base — everything below the metadata is inference from two related documents, not confirmed data for this specific device. Flagging clearly rather than presenting it as equivalent to a sourced document.

**2. Indications**
No independent FDA indication statement or study exists for this specific model. By extension from its two component technologies (documented separately in this knowledge base): likely indicated for aphakia and corneal astigmatism correction after cataract removal, with the enVista Aspire optic's useful intermediate vision. This is an inference, not a confirmed FDA or published indication for Model ETA specifically.

**3. How it works**
Per a peer-reviewed review article (Feltrin de Barros et al.'s technology is described there; see §7), the enVista Aspire optic "expands the depth of focus through a 1.5mm zone of variable curvature placed on the posterior surface" — higher-order aspheric coefficients and controlled curvature change, not a diffractive design. This model combines that same optic with a toric surface for astigmatism correction, the same general approach used in enVista Toric (adding a toric surface to the base enVista monofocal — see that document for the toric mechanism and rotation-risk profile in detail). No source independently confirms the two combine without unexpected interaction effects; this specific combination's mechanism description is architectural inference from its two component technologies, not tested-as-one data.

**4. Expected visual outcomes**
**No visual acuity outcome data exists for this specific lens.** In the absence of a dedicated study, the closest available reference points are this lens's two component technologies, documented elsewhere in this knowledge base:
- **Optic performance (by extension from enVista Aspire, non-toric):** Feltrin de Barros et al. 2025 (n=29, no comparator arm) found mean UDVA 20/22 (93.1% ≥20/25), mean CDVA 20/20 (100% ≥20/25), mean DCIVA 20/34 (90% ≥20/40 at 66cm), mean DCNVA 20/38 (83% ≥20/40 at 40cm). These figures are for the *non-toric* Aspire optic in patients without significant pre-existing astigmatism — they are not confirmed for this toric model, whose candidates by definition have astigmatism the base study population did not.
- **Astigmatism correction (by extension from enVista Toric):** in the FDA pivotal trial for enVista Toric (a different toric platform on the same manufacturer's base enVista optic, not the Aspire optic), the toric group achieved 20/20-or-better uncorrected distance vision in 45.0% of eyes vs. 16.7% for the non-toric control — illustrative of the general magnitude of benefit toric correction provides on this manufacturer's platform, not a measurement of this specific lens.

Treat both reference points as informed context, not as this lens's own trial data — flag the gap explicitly to a patient asking about this specific model.

**5. Known side effects / visual disturbances** *(flagged for Martin's review — not auto-approved)*
No visual-disturbance data exists for this specific lens. By extension: enVista Aspire's own source (Feltrin de Barros et al.) did not measure halos/glare/starbursts at all (see that document's §5 — a gap in the parent lens's evidence too, not just this one). enVista Toric's trial found a very clean safety profile with no secondary surgical interventions and no meaningful complication-rate difference vs. non-toric control — offered as the best available toric-risk reference point on this manufacturer's platform, not a measurement of this lens. General cataract-surgery risks apply. Toric-specific risk (reduced correction if the lens rotates from its intended axis) applies by extension from the toric-lens category generally. See the market-status note in §1 regarding the 2025 recall.
