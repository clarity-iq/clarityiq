# enVista Aspire — Patient Labeling

**1. Metadata**
- Lens: enVista® ASPIRE monofocal IOL (Model EA) and enVista® ASPIRE Toric IOL (Model ETA)
- Manufacturer: Bausch + Lomb, Incorporated
- Category: "Enhanced monofocal" IOL — a single-focus lens design with higher-order aspheric coefficients and controlled curvature change intended to extend useful intermediate vision beyond a standard monofocal, without diffractive multifocal technology; toric model additionally corrects corneal astigmatism
- PMA: P910056, Supplement S051 (Normal 180 Day Track, decided 2023-08-21) — introduced alongside a new "UV + Duo" lens material across the enVista portfolio
- **Market status note:** Per the master list, this lens was voluntarily recalled around April 2025 (same TASS-related recall affecting enVista Envy) and is confirmed back on the market — flagged explicitly for informed patient discussion, not omitted.
- **Source note:** No FDA SSED or patient brochure is publicly posted for this supplement. Sourced from a single peer-reviewed observational study (below) — no larger or comparator-controlled study was found in a PubMed search. This is a smaller evidence base than most other lenses in this knowledge base; flagged accordingly.

**2. Indications**
Not independently detailed in a public FDA indication statement for this supplement (the approval order only describes the material and model additions, not full indication language). Functionally, per the peer-reviewed study below and the "enhanced monofocal" category: visual correction of aphakia after cataract removal, with useful intermediate vision while maintaining excellent distance vision; the toric model additionally corrects corneal astigmatism. Capsular bag placement.

**3. How it works**
Per Feltrin de Barros et al. 2025 (J Refract Surg): a monofocal IOL using higher-order aspheric coefficients and controlled curvature change — described by the study authors as providing "intermediate optimized optics." Not a diffractive or multifocal design. Wavefront analysis in the study found minimal postoperative spherical aberration (0.08 μm at a 4.5mm pupil, 0.15 μm at 6mm).

**4. Expected visual outcomes**
From **Feltrin de Barros et al. 2025, Journal of Refractive Surgery** (prospective observational study, n=29 eyes/29 patients, healthy eyes with normal visual potential, single lens group — no monofocal comparator arm in this study):
- Uncorrected distance vision (UDVA): mean 20/22; 93.1% of eyes 20/25 or better
- Corrected distance vision (CDVA): mean 20/20; 100% of eyes 20/25 or better
- Distance-corrected intermediate vision (DCIVA, 66cm): mean 20/34; 90% of eyes 20/40 or better
- Distance-corrected near vision (DCNVA, 40cm): mean 20/38; 83% of eyes 20/40 or better
- Depth of focus: approximately 1.50D (defocus curve, 0.2 logMAR or better threshold)
- Postoperative refraction was close to emmetropia (mean spherical equivalent 0.02D ± 0.29D); 96.6% of eyes within ±0.50D of target

This is a single small study without a comparator lens — treat these as descriptive outcomes for this lens alone, not a head-to-head comparison against a standard monofocal or other enVista models. Two of the six study authors disclose consulting relationships with Bausch + Lomb — flagged for transparency.

**5. Known side effects / visual disturbances** *(flagged for Martin's review — not auto-approved)*
**No visual-disturbance data (halos, glare, starbursts) was reported in the available source** — the Feltrin de Barros study focused on optical/refractive performance, not patient-reported disturbance rates or a validated Quality-of-Vision questionnaire. This is a real gap in the current evidence base for this specific lens, not an indication that no disturbances occur — do not state or imply this lens has a favorable disturbance profile without a source to support it. General cataract-surgery risks apply, as with any IOL implantation. See the market-status note in §1 regarding the 2025 recall.

**6. Contraindications / poor-candidate factors**
Not independently established in the available sources for this lens. Standard cataract-surgery candidacy considerations apply; the toric model would require the standard toric-candidacy considerations noted for enVista Toric elsewhere in this knowledge base.
