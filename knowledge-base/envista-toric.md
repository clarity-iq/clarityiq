# enVista Toric — Patient Labeling

**1. Metadata**
- Lens: enVista® One-Piece Hydrophobic Acrylic Toric IOL, Model MX60T
- Manufacturer: Bausch + Lomb, Incorporated
- Category: Toric (astigmatism-correcting) monofocal IOL, aspheric surface enhancement — built on the same platform as base enVista
- PMA: P910056, Supplement S027 (Panel Track, decided 2018-06-08)
- Source: FDA Summary of Safety and Effectiveness Data (SSED), `accessdata.fda.gov/cdrh_docs/pdf/P910056S027B.pdf`

**2. Indications**
Primary implantation in the capsular bag for visual correction of aphakia and corneal astigmatism following cataract removal, for improved uncorrected distance vision. A monofocal — does not provide near/intermediate presbyopia-correcting vision. Capsular bag placement.

**3. How it works**
An ultraviolet-absorbing, one-piece hydrophobic acrylic astigmatism-correcting monofocal IOL — the same material and aspheric, spherical-aberration-neutral optic as base enVista, with a toric surface added. Uses an "Accuset" haptic (broad, modified C-loop design with optic-haptic offset) intended to improve contact and stability within the capsular bag, plus the same SureEdge posterior square-edge PCO barrier as the base lens. Posterior cylinder axis marks denote the correction meridian for surgical alignment. Corrects astigmatism only when positioned correctly; if the lens rotates from its intended axis, correction is reduced.

**4. Expected visual outcomes**
From the FDA pivotal trial (all Toric IOL models combined, n=112, vs. non-toric control (base enVista MX60), n=79, at the final study visit ~6 months post-op):
- Best-corrected distance vision (BCDVA): 20/40 or better in 99.1% of toric eyes (111/112) vs. 100% of control eyes — met the trial's primary safety endpoint (non-inferior to the historical control rate of 92.5%)
- Uncorrected distance vision (UCDVA): 20/40 or better in 94.5% of toric eyes vs. 83.3% of control eyes
- **20/20 or better (uncorrected): 45.0% of toric eyes (50/110) vs. 16.7% of control eyes (13/78) — a statistically significant difference (P<.01)**, supporting less reliance on glasses for distance vision
- Mean percent reduction in astigmatism (absolute cylinder): 64.8% for the 1.25D toric model, 81.0% for 2.00D, 82.8% for 2.75D, vs. 36.8% for the non-toric control (residual natural variation, since the control lens doesn't correct astigmatism)

Population-level trial data — not a guarantee of any individual patient's outcome.

**5. Known side effects / visual disturbances** *(flagged for Martin's review — not auto-approved)*
From the same trial's safety data — a notably clean safety profile in both arms: no secondary surgical interventions in either the toric group (0/112) or control group (0/79); no endophthalmitis, hypopyon, lens dislocation, pupillary block, or retinal detachment reported in any eye through the final visit. No eyes in any treatment arm lost ≥10 letters of vision from baseline at the final visit (3 eyes had a transient loss at an earlier visit that recovered). If the lens rotates from its intended axis after surgery, astigmatism correction is reduced and patients may notice visual distortion until addressed — the standard toric-lens risk, though not separately quantified as an adverse event rate in this SSED. General cataract-surgery risks apply.

**6. Contraindications / poor-candidate factors**
FDA's SSED states there are no known contraindications specific to this device. Standard toric-candidacy considerations apply (patients need a measurable, correctable amount of corneal astigmatism; conditions raising the risk of postoperative lens rotation would be a poor-candidate factor by general toric-IOL logic, though not independently confirmed as a formal contraindication in this SSED).
