# enVista — Patient Labeling

**1. Metadata**
- Lens: enVista® One-Piece Hydrophobic Acrylic IOL, Model MX60
- Manufacturer: Bausch + Lomb, Incorporated (Bausch & Lomb)
- Category: Standard monofocal IOL, aspheric optic — the base platform underlying enVista Toric, enVista Aspire, and enVista Envy in this knowledge base
- PMA: P910056, Supplement S010 (decided 2012-04-30) — a material/design modification (3-piece to 1-piece, aspheric surface) of Bausch & Lomb's earlier-approved silicone lens (C31UB), remarketed under the enVista name. The original base PMA P910056 dates to 1992 (a different, earlier silicone lens design) and is incorporated by reference.
- **Source note:** No FDA SSED or patient brochure is publicly posted for S010 itself. Indications are drawn from the FDA approval order for S010; device description and visual-outcome data are drawn from this same lens's role as the **control arm** (Model MX60, n=79) in the FDA pivotal trial for enVista Toric (PMA P910056/S027) — real FDA-reviewed clinical data about this device, sourced from a related supplement's SSED.

**2. Indications**
Primary implantation for the visual correction of aphakia in adult patients in whom the cataractous crystalline lens has been removed. A standard monofocal — does not correct pre-existing corneal astigmatism (a separate enVista Toric model exists for that) and does not provide presbyopia-correcting near/intermediate vision. Capsular bag placement.

**3. How it works**
A UV-absorbing, one-piece hydrophobic acrylic monofocal IOL. Per the enVista Toric SSED (which describes the shared platform, since enVista Toric uses "identical materials" to this lens): an aspheric optic designed to be free of spherical aberration, without altering the eye's own pre-surgical corneal spherical aberration profile. Uses a SureEdge posterior squared step-edge design intended to form a 360° posterior capsular opacification (PCO) barrier. Single fixed focal point; does not restore accommodation.

**4. Expected visual outcomes**
From this lens's role as the control arm (Model MX60, n=79) in the FDA pivotal trial for enVista Toric, at the final study visit (Form 4, ~6 months):
- Best-corrected distance vision (BCDVA): 20/40 or better in 100% of eyes (78/78 evaluable)
- Uncorrected distance vision (UCDVA): 20/40 or better in 83.3% of eyes; **20/20 or better in only 16.7% (13/78) of eyes** — for comparison, the toric version of this same platform achieved 20/20 or better in 45% of eyes in the same trial (see the enVista Toric document), illustrating the visual benefit of astigmatism correction for patients who have meaningful pre-existing astigmatism

Population-level trial data — not a guarantee of any individual patient's outcome. Presented here as this knowledge base's Bausch + Lomb monofocal baseline.

**5. Known side effects / visual disturbances** *(flagged for Martin's review — not auto-approved)*
From the same trial's control-arm safety data (N=79): essentially no meaningful complication rate — no endophthalmitis, hypopyon, lens dislocation, pupillary block, retinal detachment, or secondary surgical intervention reported in any eye through the final visit. One case (2.5%) of cystoid macular edema was reported. As a standard monofocal (non-diffractive, non-multifocal), this lens is not associated with the halo/glare/starburst profile of multifocal or trifocal designs. FDA's general list of probable adverse effects for IOL surgery (boilerplate, not specific to this trial's observed rate) includes corneal endothelial damage, infection, retinal detachment, corneal edema, pupillary block, glaucoma, and toxic anterior segment syndrome (TASS) — standard cataract-surgery risk disclosures, not findings specific to this device.

**6. Contraindications / poor-candidate factors**
FDA's SSED (for the related Toric supplement, which references this lens) states there are no known contraindications specific to this device platform. Does not correct pre-existing corneal astigmatism — patients with meaningful astigmatism would need the toric version or glasses to correct residual astigmatism after surgery.
