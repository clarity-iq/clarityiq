# TECNIS Eyhance — Patient Labeling

**1. Metadata**
- Lens: TECNIS Eyhance® IOL (Model ICB00), with SmartLOAD® Delivery Technology (Model GIB00), and with TECNIS Simplicity® Delivery System (Model DIB00); TECNIS Eyhance® Toric II IOLs (Models ICU150–600, DIU150–600)
- Manufacturer: Johnson & Johnson Surgical Vision, Inc.
- Category: "Enhanced monofocal" IOL — a single-focus lens with an optical modification that extends functional intermediate vision beyond a standard monofocal, without the diffractive multifocal/EDOF technology used in Synergy, Odyssey, Symfony, or PanOptix; Toric II models additionally correct corneal astigmatism
- PMA: P980040, Supplement S117 (decided 2020-12-29). FDA's approval order describes it only as "an extension of the TECNIS family of one-piece IOLs that incorporate an optical modification," without further technical detail.
- **Source note:** No FDA SSED or patient brochure is publicly posted for this supplement. Per the same approach used for Odyssey and Vivity, sourced instead from a pivotal randomized controlled trial published in *Journal of Cataract & Refractive Surgery* (JCRS) — the same top-tier journal used for Vivity — plus a second smaller JCRS study for supporting detail.

**2. Indications**
Visual correction of aphakia in adults after cataract removal, with improved intermediate vision compared to a standard monofocal while maintaining comparable distance vision. Toric II models additionally correct corneal astigmatism. No detailed FDA indication statement (of the kind available for Synergy/PureSee) is publicly posted for this supplement; described here consistent with FDA's brief approval order and the peer-reviewed trial's framing. Capsular bag placement.

**3. How it works**
Described in the peer-reviewed literature as a "higher-order aspheric" monofocal design — a modification of the standard TECNIS 1-Piece (ZCB00) platform's anterior optic surface, not a diffractive design. Surface profilometry studies found its surface geometry similar to a conventional monofocal lens, with only minimal differences in the central optic design (unlike the diffractive-ring or echelette patterns used in EDOF/multifocal lenses). This refractive, non-diffractive approach is the basis for its "enhanced monofocal" category, positioned between a standard monofocal and a true EDOF lens (like Symfony or PureSee) in terms of range of vision. No FDA device-description text is publicly posted for this supplement; this description is drawn from the peer-reviewed studies, not FDA labeling.

**4. Expected visual outcomes**
From **Auffarth et al. 2021, JCRS** (multicenter, prospective, randomized, evaluator-masked controlled trial — the pivotal trial for this lens; Quantum Study Group; Eyhance ICB00 n=67 vs. standard monofocal ZCB00 n=72, bilaterally implanted, 6 months): the enhanced monofocal significantly improved mean monocular and binocular distance-corrected intermediate visual acuity (DCIVA) and uncorrected intermediate visual acuity (UIVA) by at least 1 line of vision vs. the standard monofocal (P≤.0001 for both). Distance vision was 20/20 or better and comparable between groups. Contrast sensitivity, photic phenomena, and adverse event rates were similar between groups. (Note: the published abstract reports these findings in line/significance terms rather than exact logMAR means; exact figures were not available without full-text access.)

A second study, **Mencucci et al. 2020, JCRS** (prospective case series, n=80 eyes/40 patients, Eyhance ICB00 vs. TECNIS ZCB00 monofocal, 6 months): uncorrected and corrected distance and near visual acuities were similar between the two lenses, but monocular and binocular uncorrected intermediate visual acuity (UIVA) were significantly higher in the Eyhance group, which also provided better spectacle independence for intermediate distance than the standard monofocal.

Population-level trial data — not a guarantee of any individual patient's outcome.

**5. Known side effects / visual disturbances** *(flagged for Martin's review — not auto-approved)*
Both available studies found Eyhance's disturbance profile essentially indistinguishable from a standard monofocal — consistent with its non-diffractive design:
- Auffarth et al. 2021 (JCRS, n=139): "photic phenomena outcomes... were similar between the 2 groups" (Eyhance vs. standard monofocal); rates of adverse events were also similar between groups
- Mencucci et al. 2020 (JCRS, n=80 eyes): "no statistically significant differences between the 2 groups in terms of... glare and halo perception," alongside no significant differences in objective scatter index or contrast sensitivity
- Neither study reported exact halo/glare/starburst percentages in the available abstracts — for comparison, the standard TECNIS 1-Piece monofocal's own trial-measured rates are documented in this knowledge base's TECNIS 1-Piece entry, and Eyhance's rates were reported as statistically indistinguishable from that baseline
- General cataract-surgery risks apply

**6. Contraindications / poor-candidate factors**
Not independently established by FDA for this supplement (no public SSED). No exclusion criteria specific to Eyhance were detailed in the available study abstracts. General monofocal-category precautions apply (does not correct pre-existing corneal astigmatism in the non-toric model; standard cataract-surgery candidacy considerations).
