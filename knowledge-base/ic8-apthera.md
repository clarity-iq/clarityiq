# IC-8 Apthera — Patient Labeling

**1. Metadata**
- Lens: IC-8® Apthera™ IOL
- Manufacturer: AcuFocus, Inc. (distributed by Bausch + Lomb)
- Category: Small-aperture (pinhole) extended depth of focus IOL — a fundamentally different approach from the diffractive/refractive EDOF and multifocal lenses elsewhere in this knowledge base. Implanted **unilaterally only**, in the non-dominant eye, to create planned monovision alongside a standard monofocal or monofocal toric lens in the other (dominant) eye.
- PMA: P210005 (original approval, decided 2022-07-22)
- Source: FDA Summary of Safety and Effectiveness Data (SSED), `accessdata.fda.gov/cdrh_docs/pdf21/P210005B.pdf`

**2. Indications**
Unilateral implantation for visual correction of aphakia and to create monovision, in patients age 22 or older with bilateral operable cataract, up to 1.5D of astigmatism in the implanted eye, and **no history of retinal disease and no predisposition to future retinal disease**. Implanted in the capsular bag of the non-dominant eye only, and only **after** the other (dominant) eye has already been successfully implanted with a monofocal or monofocal toric IOL targeted for emmetropia (with uncorrected distance vision of 20/32 or better and best-corrected distance vision of 20/25 or better in that eye). The IC-8 eye's refractive target is -0.75D (mild myopic defocus, working with the pinhole aperture to extend depth of focus). This is a two-step, sequenced surgical plan — not a same-day bilateral procedure like most other lenses in this knowledge base.

**3. How it works**
A one-piece, UV-blocking, foldable aspheric monofocal IOL with a centrally embedded "FilterRing" mask — a small-aperture pinhole, not a diffractive or refractive multifocal design. The FilterRing (made of polyvinylidene fluoride with carbon nanoparticles) has an outer diameter of 3.23mm and an inner (aperture) diameter of 1.36mm, with 3,200 microperforations (7–10μm) arranged to maintain filter integrity. The small aperture works like a pinhole camera, extending depth of focus by blocking unfocused peripheral light rays while letting focused central rays through — the same optical principle behind squinting to see more clearly. The anterior optic surface is aspheric with negative spherical aberration; the posterior surface has a 360° square edge.

**4. Expected visual outcomes**
From the FDA pivotal trial (IC-8 group, IC-8 eye + fellow monofocal eye, n=335 vs. Control group, both eyes monofocal, n=100, at 6 months, tested with a +0.75D lens to simulate the intended monovision target in both groups):
- Distance-corrected intermediate vision (66cm), binocular: 20/25 or better in 83.0% of IC-8 group subjects vs. 56.0% of control subjects; 20/20 or better in 56.1% vs. 18.0%
- Distance-corrected near vision (40cm), binocular: 20/40 or better in 89.6% of IC-8 group subjects vs. 58.0% of control subjects; 20/20 or better in 11.0% vs. 1.0%
- Distance-corrected distance vision, binocular: 20/25 or better in 71.3% of IC-8 group subjects vs. 56.0% of control subjects; 20/40 or better in 98.8% vs. 99.0% (distance vision preserved, not traded away)
- All pre-specified performance targets for intermediate and near vision were met

Population-level trial data — not a guarantee of any individual patient's outcome. Because this lens is deliberately implanted with a myopic (-0.75D) target in one eye, the way it's evaluated (and the way outcomes should be explained to patients) is different from the same-day bilateral emmetropic-target lenses elsewhere in this knowledge base.

**5. Known side effects / visual disturbances** *(flagged for Martin's review — not auto-approved)*
From the same trial's Quality of Vision questionnaire at 12 months (IC-8 group N=331, Control N=100) — more than 80% of subjects in both groups reported "never experienced" or only mildly bothered by visual symptoms overall, but rates were meaningfully higher in the IC-8 group for specific symptoms:
- Starbursts rated "severe": 3.6% (12/331) IC-8 vs. 1.0% (1/100) control
- Halos rated "severe": 3.6% (12/331) IC-8 vs. 0.0% control
- Glare rated "severe": 3.0% (10/331) IC-8 vs. 0.0% control
- **Two IC-8 IOL explants occurred during the study** (out of the enrolled population) — one for a subjective "hinged blob" sensation (cause unclear; investigator considered posterior vitreous detachment, a capsule remnant, or a YAG laser capsulotomy artifact on the aperture as possible causes) and one specifically for starburst/glare/halo complaints. No IC-8 IOL removals were reported during the main study period itself (these two occurred among previously-enrolled subjects tracked after study exit). One control-group subject had both monofocal IOLs replaced due to dysphotopsia complaints.
- General cataract-surgery risks apply. Adverse event rates for standard complications (cystoid macular edema, iritis, raised IOP, corneal edema) were low (each ≤0.6%) and similar between groups.

**6. Contraindications / poor-candidate factors**
This is one of the few lenses in this knowledge base with a **formal FDA contraindications list**, not just general precautions:
1. Patients with dilated pupil size less than 7.0mm
2. Patients with a history of retinal disease (including but not limited to high myopia, diabetes, macular disease, sickle cell disease, retinal tear, retinal detachment, retinal vein occlusion, ocular tumor, uveitis) or who are predisposed to retinal disease in the future

Also not appropriate for patients who haven't already had successful monofocal (or monofocal toric) implantation in the fellow eye first, or whose fellow eye doesn't meet the trial's distance-vision benchmarks. Not indicated for astigmatism greater than 1.5D in the implanted eye.
