# LAL+ — Patient Labeling

**1. Metadata**
- Lens: Light Adjustable Lens Plus (LAL+), used with the same Light Delivery Device (LDD) as the original LAL
- Manufacturer: RxSight, Inc.
- Category: Post-operatively adjustable IOL — FDA's approval order describes LAL+ only as "a level A modification of the optical design of the original LAL," without specifying what the optical change is
- PMA: P160055, Supplement S024 (Normal 180 Day Track, decided 2023-03-31)
- **Source note:** No FDA SSED or patient brochure is publicly posted for this supplement, and **no dedicated peer-reviewed clinical study of LAL+ specifically was found** despite multiple PubMed search strategies (direct term, "RxSight LAL Plus," "extended range"/"extended depth" combinations). The only relevant hits were broad review articles that mention "light-adjustable lenses" as a category without LAL+-specific data. This is the second entry in this knowledge base (after enVista Aspire Toric) with essentially no independent outcome data — flagging clearly rather than presenting borrowed data as this lens's own.

**2. Indications**
No independent FDA indication statement is publicly available for this supplement. FDA's later supplement history shows LAL+ sharing the same regulatory framework as the original LAL (S038, 2024, extended LAL+'s spherical power range without adding a new indication), suggesting LAL+'s FDA-cleared use remains the same as the base LAL — reduction of residual astigmatism and spherical refractive error via postoperative light adjustment — rather than a formally distinct presbyopia-correcting indication. This is inference from the supplement history, not a confirmed indication statement for LAL+ specifically.

**3. How it works**
By extension from the base LAL (see that document for the full mechanism): a photoreactive silicone IOL whose power is adjusted after implantation using targeted UV light from the Light Delivery Device, then permanently locked in. FDA's record confirms LAL+ has a modified optical design relative to the original LAL ("Level A modification") but does not describe what changed. Some public reporting on RxSight's product line describes LAL+ as intended to extend depth of focus relative to the original LAL, but **this has not been independently confirmed against an FDA record or peer-reviewed source** in this research — do not state this as an established fact without a better source.

**4. Expected visual outcomes**
**No visual acuity outcome data exists for LAL+ specifically.** The original LAL's own trial data (documented in this knowledge base's Light Adjustable Lens entry — 70.1% of eyes achieving 20/20-or-better uncorrected distance vision vs. 36.3% for monofocal control, and comparable astigmatism-reduction rates) is for a different, though related, lens design and should not be cited as established for LAL+.

**5. Known side effects / visual disturbances** *(flagged for Martin's review — not auto-approved)*
No LAL+-specific disturbance or adverse-event data exists. By extension, LAL+ shares the same core UV-adjustment mechanism and Light Delivery Device as the original LAL, so the device-specific risk categories documented for that lens — phototoxic retinal damage risk (tied to LDD equipment function, not the optic design change), tritan color vision anomaly, erythropsia, and an elevated secondary-surgical-intervention rate relative to standard IOLs — are the best available reference point, not a confirmed measurement of LAL+ itself. See the Light Adjustable Lens document in this knowledge base for the specific rates observed in that trial.

**6. Contraindications / poor-candidate factors**
Not independently confirmed for LAL+ specifically, but the same UV-light-treatment mechanism strongly implies the same contraindication list applies: UV-sensitizing medications, retina-toxic medications (e.g., tamoxifen), history of ocular herpes simplex, nystagmus, and unwillingness/inability to complete the adjustment/lock-in visit schedule and wear UV-protective eyewear. Treat as inference from the shared mechanism, not an independently confirmed LAL+ contraindications list.
