# Clareon PanOptix Pro — Patient Labeling

**1. Metadata**
- Lens: Clareon® PanOptix® Pro Trifocal IOL (Model PXYWT0, non-toric) and Clareon® PanOptix® Pro Trifocal Toric IOLs (Models PXYWT3–PXYWT6), plus UV-absorbing and AutonoMe pre-loaded delivery variants
- Manufacturer: Alcon Laboratories, LLC
- Category: Trifocal (multifocal), presbyopia-correcting IOL; toric models additionally correct corneal astigmatism
- PMA: P190018, Supplement S029 (received 2023-09-27, decided/approved 2023-12-18)
- FDA characterization of this approval: *"a minor optical modification of the approved Clareon PanOptix models"* — approved via FDA's Real-Time Process supplement pathway (design/component/material change), not as a new device requiring independent clinical trials
- Source: FDA openFDA structured PMA record (`api.fda.gov/device/pma.json`, `pma_number=P190018`, `supplement_number=S029`)

**2. Indications**
Same population and use as the base Clareon PanOptix (see that document) — capsular bag implantation to replace the natural lens after cataract removal in adults, with toric models for patients who also need corneal astigmatism correction.

**3. What's actually different from base PanOptix**
FDA's own record describes this only as a "minor optical modification" and doesn't detail the technical mechanism. The following description of what changed is **sourced from Alcon's own technical/marketing materials, not the FDA record** — flagging that distinction explicitly since the rest of this document's other sections rely on FDA source data and this section does not:

Pro uses what Alcon calls "ENLIGHTEN NXT" optical technology — a refinement of the diffractive design used in base PanOptix. Per Alcon's published technical claims: ~94% light utilization (vs. ~88% for base PanOptix), roughly half the light scatter, and a claimed 16% increase in optical image contrast between distance and intermediate vision. Alcon describes this as recovering roughly 50% of the light previously lost to scatter and redirecting it to a beneficial focal point, via a 4.5mm diffractive zone intended to reduce dependence on pupil size across lighting conditions.
