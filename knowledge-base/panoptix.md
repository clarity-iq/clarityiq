# PanOptix — Patient Labeling

**1. Metadata**
- Lens: AcrySof® IQ PanOptix® Trifocal IOL (Model TFNT00, non-toric) and AcrySof® IQ PanOptix® Toric Trifocal IOL (Models TFNT30/40/50/60)
- Manufacturer: Alcon Laboratories, Inc.
- Category: Trifocal (multifocal), presbyopia-correcting IOL; toric models additionally correct corneal astigmatism
- PMA: P040020, Supplement S087
- Source: FDA Patient Information Brochure, `accessdata.fda.gov/cdrh_docs/pdf4/P040020S087C.pdf`

**2. Indications**
Implanted in the capsular bag to replace the natural lens after cataract removal, in adults. Model TFNT00 does not correct corneal astigmatism and is indicated for patients without significant pre-existing astigmatism. The toric models (TFNT30–60) are for patients who also need corneal astigmatism correction, selected by the surgeon based on the amount of astigmatism present.

**3. How it works**
A trifocal design — the center of the lens is engineered to provide near (reading) and intermediate (computer-distance) vision in addition to distance vision, unlike a standard monofocal lens which focuses at only one distance. All five models provide similar distance vision and better intermediate/near vision than a monofocal IOL. Full benefit requires implantation in both eyes.

**4. Expected visual outcomes**
From the FDA pivotal trial (TFNT00 vs. monofocal control, both eyes tested together, 6 months post-op):
- Distance vision without glasses, ≥20/25: 92% (PanOptix) vs. 100% (monofocal)
- Intermediate vision without glasses, ≥20/25: 94% (PanOptix) vs. 51% (monofocal)
- Near vision without glasses, ≥20/25: 92% (PanOptix) vs. 9% (monofocal)
- Never needed glasses post-op (any distance): 80.5% (PanOptix, n=129) vs. 8.2% (monofocal, n=114)

Population-level trial data — not a guarantee of any individual patient's outcome.

**5. Known side effects / visual disturbances** *(flagged for Martin's review — not auto-approved)*
At 6 months, rates of patients rating the disturbance as severe: halos 13%, starbursts 16%, glare 3% (vs. 1%, 2%, 2% for monofocal). Rates rating themselves "bothered very much" were substantially lower: halos 2%, starbursts 5%, glare 2%. Contrast sensitivity was slightly reduced versus monofocal. Multifocal-related visual disturbances (halos/glare/starbursts) can make night driving and low-light tasks harder than before surgery. For toric models specifically: if the lens isn't positioned correctly or rotates after surgery, astigmatism correction is reduced and a second procedure may be needed to reposition it.

**6. Contraindications / poor-candidate factors**
Irregular astigmatism (irregular corneal shape). Large amounts of astigmatism before surgery if choosing the non-toric model, or large remaining astigmatism after surgery for either model. Safety and effectiveness not established in patients with glaucoma or diabetic retinopathy. Pre-existing retinal conditions (macular degeneration, diabetic edema) may reduce or negate the visual benefit of a multifocal lens specifically.

**7. Source citation**
PMA P040020, Supplement S087 (approved as an amendment to Alcon's original AcrySof multifocal-platform PMA). Patient Information Brochure: `https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/cdrh_docs/pdf4/P040020S087C.pdf`
