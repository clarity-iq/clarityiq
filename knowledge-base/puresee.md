# TECNIS PureSee — Patient Labeling

**1. Metadata**
- Lens: TECNIS PureSee® IOL with TECNIS Simplicity® Delivery System (Model DEN00V, non-toric) and TECNIS PureSee® Toric II IOLs with TECNIS Simplicity® Delivery System (Models DET150/225/300/375)
- Manufacturer: Johnson & Johnson Surgical Vision, Inc.
- Category: Extended depth of focus (EDOF) IOL, purely refractive design (not diffractive) — closer in mechanism to an enhanced monofocal than to Symfony/Synergy/Odyssey; toric models additionally correct corneal astigmatism
- PMA: P980040, Supplement S176 (received 2025-07-31, decided/approved 2026-03-11)
- Source: FDA Summary of Safety and Effectiveness Data (SSED), `accessdata.fda.gov/cdrh_docs/pdf/P980040S176B.pdf`

**2. Indications**
Model DEN00V: primary implantation for visual correction of aphakia in adults with less than 1 diopter of pre-existing corneal astigmatism, after cataract removal. Mitigates presbyopia via extended depth of focus, improving intermediate visual acuity vs. an aspheric monofocal while maintaining comparable distance visual acuity. Toric II models (DET150–375): same population plus reduction of refractive astigmatism, for patients with ≥1 diopter of preoperative corneal astigmatism. Capsular bag placement only for all models.

**3. How it works**
A UV/violet-light-absorbing posterior chamber IOL providing extended depth of focus via a **purely refractive** design — not a diffractive one. The posterior optic uses proprietary "OptiCurve" technology to create a modulated power profile that extends depth of focus, while the anterior optic is wavefront-designed aspheric (or toric-aspheric) to compensate for corneal spherical aberration. FDA's device description specifically notes the lens is designed to provide improved intermediate vision "while maintaining distance visual acuity, contrast sensitivity, and low levels of visual symptoms" — i.e., the refractive (non-diffractive) approach is intended to avoid the light-splitting tradeoffs of diffractive multifocal/EDOF lenses. Pupil-independent performance across tested pupil sizes. Does not restore accommodation.

**4. Expected visual outcomes**
From the FDA pivotal trial (PureSee n=113–116 vs. monofocal control n=110–112 depending on analysis population, 6 months post-op):
- Distance vision (best-corrected, monocular): mean 20/18 Snellen (PureSee) vs. 20/16 Snellen (control) — both groups saw better than 20/20 on average; non-inferior, meeting the trial's success criterion. **20/20 or better: 80.5% of PureSee eyes vs. 88.2% of control eyes** (binocularly, 93.8% vs. 97.3%) — a modest, expected tradeoff for the intermediate-vision gain, similar in pattern to Synergy and Symfony
- Intermediate vision at 66cm, distance-corrected (first co-primary effectiveness endpoint): mean 20/28 Snellen (PureSee) vs. 20/40 Snellen (control) — 1.5-line improvement, P<.0001. **20/20 or better (binocular): 31.0% of PureSee eyes vs. 10.9% of control eyes**
- 20/32 or better intermediate vision (distance-corrected): 82.3% (PureSee) vs. 30.0% (control) — a 52.3 percentage-point difference
- Spectacle wear ("none/a little of the time," overall vision): 74.4% (PureSee) vs. 50.0% (control); for intermediate vision specifically, "none of the time": 77.9% vs. 62.7%
- Near vision spectacle independence was more modest than intermediate: "none of the time" for near vision was 26.5% (PureSee) vs. 8.2% (control) — consistent with this lens prioritizing distance-through-intermediate range over near, unlike the multifocal designs (PanOptix, Synergy, Odyssey) in this knowledge base

Population-level trial data — not a guarantee of any individual patient's outcome.

**5. Known side effects / visual disturbances** *(flagged for Martin's review — not auto-approved)*
This trial's disturbance rates were notably close between PureSee and the monofocal control — consistent with the purely refractive (non-diffractive) design intent described in §3:
- Directed questionnaire, 6 months: halos "never" 73.5% (PureSee) vs. 68.2% (control); starbursts "never" 70.8% vs. 73.6%; among those reporting halos/glare/starbursts, only 3 PureSee subjects (of 113) rated each symptom "very or extremely bothered"
- Spontaneous reports at 6 months: halos 5.3% (PureSee) vs. 4.5% (control); night glare 4.4% vs. 1.8%; starbursts 5.3% vs. 0.9% — none of the PureSee subjects reported severe halos, night glare, or starbursts (one control subject reported severe halos)
- Most frequently reported symptoms in both groups were sensitivity to light and poor low-light vision, not halos/starbursts/glare — sensitivity to light "sometimes or more" in 38 PureSee subjects vs. 37 control; poor low-light vision in 40 PureSee vs. 25 control
- Contrast sensitivity: no clinically meaningful differences (≤0.3 log units) between PureSee and control across pupil sizes or lighting conditions tested

**6. Contraindications / poor-candidate factors**
FDA's SSED states there are no known contraindications specific to this device. General cataract-surgery precautions apply. Because this lens prioritizes distance/intermediate vision with more modest near-vision spectacle independence (see §4), patients whose primary goal is glasses-free reading may be better served by a multifocal design (e.g., PanOptix, Synergy) — a candidacy/preference consideration, not an FDA-confirmed contraindication.
