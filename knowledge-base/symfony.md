# TECNIS Symfony — Patient Labeling

**1. Metadata**
- Lens: TECNIS® Symfony Extended Range of Vision IOL, Model ZXR00 (non-toric), and TECNIS® Symfony Toric Extended Range of Vision IOLs, Models ZXT150/225/300/375
- Manufacturer: Johnson & Johnson Surgical Vision, Inc. (approved under prior corporate name, Abbott Medical Optics Inc.)
- Category: Extended depth of focus (EDOF) IOL, non-diffractive-multifocal design; toric models additionally correct corneal astigmatism
- PMA: P980040, Supplement S065 (approved 2016-07-15)
- Source: FDA Summary of Safety and Effectiveness Data (SSED), `accessdata.fda.gov/cdrh_docs/pdf/P980040S065B.pdf`

**2. Indications**
Model ZXR00: primary implantation for visual correction of aphakia in adults with less than 1 diopter of pre-existing corneal astigmatism, after cataract removal. Mitigates presbyopia via extended depth of focus, improving intermediate and near acuity vs. an aspheric monofocal while maintaining comparable distance vision. Toric models (ZXT150–375): same population plus reduction of residual refractive astigmatism, for patients with ≥1 diopter of preoperative corneal astigmatism. Capsular bag placement only for all models.

**3. How it works**
A UV-absorbing posterior chamber IOL intended to provide a continuous range of vision without compromising distance vision (toric models additionally correct astigmatism). The anterior optic is wavefront-designed aspheric (or toric-aspheric), correcting corneal spherical aberration; a frosted, squared posterior edge is designed to reduce edge glare. The posterior optic has a proprietary achromatic diffractive surface with an "echelette" design intended to correct chromatic aberration and extend the range of vision (far, intermediate, near) while preserving the spherical-aberration correction. Designed for pupil-independent performance in any lighting condition. This is a non-diffractive-multifocal EDOF design — distinct from PanOptix's trifocal or Synergy/Odyssey's combined EDOF-multifocal approach.

**4. Expected visual outcomes**
From the FDA pivotal trial (Symfony n=147 vs. monofocal control n=148, monocular unless noted, 6 months post-op):
- Intermediate vision at 66cm, uncorrected: mean 20/25 Snellen (Symfony) vs. 20/40 Snellen (control) — 1.7-line improvement, P<.0001; met primary effectiveness endpoint. **20/20 or better: 40.8% of Symfony eyes vs. 12.8% of control eyes.**
- Intermediate vision at 66cm, distance-corrected: mean 20/25 Snellen (Symfony) vs. 20/40 Snellen (control) — 2.4-line improvement, P<.0001. **20/20 or better: 34.7% of Symfony eyes vs. 4.7% of control eyes.**
- 20/25 or better intermediate (uncorrected): 76.9% (Symfony) vs. 33.8% (control); distance-corrected: 70.1% vs. 13.5%
- Near vision at 40cm, distance-corrected: 2.2-line improvement over control (P<.0001); 61.9% of Symfony eyes achieved 20/40 or better vs. 16.2% of control eyes; binocularly, 90.5% of Symfony eyes achieved 20/40 or better vs. 34.5% of control eyes
- Distance vision (best-corrected): 20/20 or better in 83.7% of Symfony eyes vs. 88.5% of control eyes (binocular: 93.2% vs. 95.3%) — a modest, expected tradeoff for the intermediate/near gains, comparable to the pattern seen with Synergy
- Spectacle wear ("none of the time" or "a little of the time," overall vision): 85.0% (Symfony) vs. 59.9% (control), P<.0001; wearing glasses "none of the time": 62.6% vs. 32.0%

Population-level trial data — not a guarantee of any individual patient's outcome. Note: the uncorrected and distance-corrected intermediate mean acuities both round to the same nominal Snellen line (20/25 vs. 20/40) despite reflecting different underlying logMAR means — Snellen lines are coarse, discrete steps, which is why the 20/20-or-better percentages above (rather than the mean line alone) are the more informative comparison.

**5. Known side effects / visual disturbances** *(flagged for Martin's review — not auto-approved)*
From the same trial's directed questionnaire at 6 months (Symfony N=147, control N=148) — halos and starbursts were reported with increased bother in the Symfony group vs. monofocal:
- Halos: no bother 40.8% (vs. 70.9% control); "very bothered" 6.8% (vs. 2.7%)
- Starbursts: no bother 42.2% (vs. 74.3% control); "very bothered" 8.2% (vs. 4.1%)
- Glare: no bother 42.8% (vs. 57.4% control); "very bothered" 5.5% (vs. 3.4%)
- Streaks of light and shadow/occlusion complaints were similar between groups (no meaningful increase for Symfony)
- Spontaneous (non-directed) reports at 6 months: halos 16.3% vs. 1.4% control (severe 2.7% vs. 0.7%); night glare 2.7% vs. 0%; starbursts 8.8% vs. 1.4% (severe 1.4% vs. 0%)
- FDA notes non-directed (spontaneous) reports are generally lower and less reliable than directed-questionnaire rates — both are shown above for completeness

**6. Contraindications / poor-candidate factors**
FDA's SSED states there are no known contraindications specific to this device. General cataract-surgery precautions and the multifocal/EDOF-category cautions (pre-existing retinal disease, irregular astigmatism affecting benefit) apply by extension, consistent with the other EDOF/multifocal lenses in this knowledge base.
