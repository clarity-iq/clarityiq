# TECNIS Synergy — Patient Labeling

**1. Metadata**
- Lens: TECNIS Synergy® Intraocular Lens (Model ZFR00V) and TECNIS Synergy® IOL with TECNIS Simplicity® Delivery System (Model DFR00V), non-toric
- Manufacturer: Johnson & Johnson Surgical Vision, Inc.
- Category: Continuous-range-of-vision multifocal IOL (J&J describes it as combining extended depth of focus and multifocal diffractive technology)
- PMA: P980040, Supplement S124 (received 2020-10-02, decided/approved 2021-04-28)
- Source: FDA Summary of Safety and Effectiveness Data (SSED), `accessdata.fda.gov/cdrh_docs/pdf/P980040S124B.pdf`. Note: unlike the Alcon lenses documented earlier, no separate FDA patient information brochure is listed for this supplement — the SSED itself contains the clinical outcome and device-description data used below.
- Scope note: this document covers the non-toric Synergy model only. The pivotal clinical trial's inclusion criteria required predicted postoperative corneal astigmatism under 1.00D, so all outcome data below applies to the non-toric lens. The toric version (TECNIS Synergy Toric II) shares the same approval (P980040/S124) but was not part of this trial cohort — it will be documented separately under the shared "Toric II" platform entry per the master list.

**2. Indications**
Primary implantation for the visual correction of aphakia in adult patients with less than 1 diopter of pre-existing corneal astigmatism, in whom a cataractous lens has been removed. Compared to an aspheric monofocal lens, mitigates the effects of presbyopia by improving visual acuity at intermediate and near distances while maintaining comparable distance visual acuity. Capsular bag placement only.

**3. How it works**
An ultraviolet- and violet-light-absorbing posterior chamber IOL. The posterior optic surface has a proprietary diffractive design combining extended depth of focus (EDF) and multifocal technologies, providing continuous 20/32-or-better vision from optical infinity through at least -3.0D of defocus (roughly infinity to 33cm) — far vision comparable to a monofocal lens, plus meaningfully improved intermediate and near vision, including in low light. The anterior optic surface is wavefront-designed aspheric, correcting corneal spherical aberration. The squared posterior edge has a frosted design intended to reduce edge glare. Performance was shown to be pupil-independent across the pupil sizes tested (>2.5mm). Does not restore natural accommodation.

**4. Expected visual outcomes**
From the FDA pivotal trial (Synergy n=135 vs. monofocal control n=137, first eyes, 6 months post-op; 131 Synergy and 131 control eyes analyzed):
- Near vision at 40cm (monocular, distance-corrected): 0.104 logMAR (Synergy) vs. 0.522 logMAR (monofocal) — a 4.2-line improvement, P<.0001; met the trial's primary effectiveness endpoint
- Near vision at 33cm (monocular, distance-corrected): 0.154 logMAR (Synergy) vs. 0.608 logMAR (monofocal) — a 4.5-line improvement, P<.0001
- Distance vision: comparable between groups (non-inferiority endpoint met)
- Spectacle independence: 87.8% of Synergy patients (115/131) reported wearing glasses "none of the time" across all four conditions (distance, intermediate, near, overall vision) at 6 months, vs. 3.1% of monofocal patients (4/131) — an 84.7 percentage-point difference, P<.0001

Population-level trial data — not a guarantee of any individual patient's outcome.

**5. Known side effects / visual disturbances** *(flagged for Martin's review — not auto-approved)*
From the same trial's directed visual-symptom questionnaire at 6 months (Synergy N=131, monofocal N=131):
- Halos: 18.3% did not experience it (vs. 60.3% monofocal); of those who did, ratings ranged from "not at all bothered" (14.5%) to "extremely bothered" (3.8%)
- Starbursts: 35.1% did not experience it (vs. 77.9% monofocal); ratings ranged from "not at all bothered" (13.7%) to "extremely bothered" (5.3%)
- Glare related to scattered light: 52.7% did not experience it (vs. 84.0% monofocal); "extremely bothered" 4.6%
- Spontaneous (non-directed) reports at 6 months: halos 22.9% vs. 3.8% monofocal (severe: 3.8% vs. 0%); night glare 8.4% vs. 1.5% (severe: 2.3% vs. 0%); starbursts 9.9% vs. 1.5% (severe: 3.8% vs. 0%)
- Extremely-bothersome ratings were higher for Synergy across halos (3.8%), glare (4.6%), and starbursts (5.3%) than for the monofocal control
- Contrast sensitivity was lower for Synergy than control under glare conditions tested; FDA's review concluded these differences "did not appear to be clinically meaningful"
- One secondary surgical intervention related to the lens's optical properties occurred during the study; adverse event rates were otherwise below or not statistically higher than ISO 11979-7 safety/performance benchmark rates

**6. Contraindications / poor-candidate factors**
FDA's SSED states there are no known contraindications specific to this device. Trial exclusion criteria (useful as poor-candidate signals, not formal contraindications) included: prior corneal refractive or intraocular surgery, corneal dystrophies predicted to limit vision below 20/30, degenerative retinal disease (e.g., macular degeneration) predicted to limit vision below 20/30, conditions associated with zonular/capsular instability (e.g., pseudoexfoliation, trauma), uncontrolled glaucoma, diabetic retinopathy, and predicted postoperative corneal astigmatism of 1.00D or more (these patients would instead be candidates for the toric version).
