# TECNIS 1-Piece — Patient Labeling

**1. Metadata**
- Lens: TECNIS® 1-Piece IOL, Model ZCB00 (standard configuration) and Model DCB00 (with TECNIS Simplicity® Delivery System)
- Manufacturer: Johnson & Johnson Surgical Vision, Inc.
- Category: Standard monofocal IOL with an aspheric optic (single fixed focal point, no presbyopia-correcting technology) — the base platform underlying several other lenses in this knowledge base (Eyhance, Synergy, Odyssey, PureSee all describe themselves as design modifications of this lens or use it as their monofocal comparator)
- PMA: P980040, Supplement S015 (original approval of Model ZCB00, decided 2007-10-30)
- **Source note:** No FDA SSED or patient brochure is publicly posted for S015 (it predates FDA's routine online posting of these documents). Per the same FDA-first approach used throughout this knowledge base, indications and device description below are drawn from the FDA approval order for S015; visual acuity and side-effect data are drawn from this same lens's role as the **monofocal control arm** in the FDA-regulated pivotal trial for TECNIS PureSee (PMA P980040/S176) — i.e., real FDA-reviewed clinical-trial data about this device, sourced from a different supplement's SSED rather than one of its own.

**2. Indications**
Visual correction of aphakia in adults after cataract removal, restoring distance vision. Per the PureSee SSED's direct comparison: "a legally marketed alternative with similar indications for use, except that it is not intended to provide improved vision at intermediate distance" — i.e., a standard single-focus lens, not a presbyopia-correcting design. Does not correct pre-existing corneal astigmatism (a separate Toric 1-Piece model exists for that). Capsular bag placement.

**3. How it works**
A single-piece, UV-absorbing, aspheric acrylic monofocal IOL. FDA's original 2007 approval order for this model describes it as adding a surface treatment to the acrylic material and a wavefront-designed aspheric optic (vs. the earlier 3-piece SENSAR parent lens), intended to reduce postoperative spherical aberration and improve night-driving simulator performance. Provides a single fixed focal point at distance; does not provide intermediate or near vision the way EDOF/multifocal lenses do, and does not restore accommodation.

**4. Expected visual outcomes**
From this lens's role as the monofocal control (Model DCB00) in the FDA pivotal trial for TECNIS PureSee (n=110–112, 6 months post-op, same trial documented in the PureSee entry in this knowledge base):
- Best-corrected distance vision (monocular): mean 20/16–20/17 Snellen; 20/20 or better in 88.2% of eyes (binocularly, 97.3%) — comparable, slightly better on this specific measure than the presbyopia-correcting lenses built on this platform, consistent with a standard monofocal's tradeoff-free distance focus
- Intermediate vision at 66cm, distance-corrected: mean 20/40 Snellen; 20/20 or better (binocular) in only 10.9% of eyes — meaningfully worse than PureSee, Synergy, Odyssey, or Symfony, consistent with this lens providing no intermediate-range benefit
- Spectacle wear ("none of the time," overall vision): 31.8% — most patients with this lens continue to need glasses for non-distance tasks, which is the expected tradeoff for a standard monofocal

Population-level trial data — not a guarantee of any individual patient's outcome. Presented here as the baseline this knowledge base's other J&J lenses are measured against.

**5. Known side effects / visual disturbances** *(flagged for Martin's review — not auto-approved)*
From the same PureSee trial's control-arm data (N=110):
- Directed questionnaire, 6 months: halos "never" 68.2%; starbursts "never" 73.6%
- Spontaneous reports at 6 months: halos 4.5%, night glare 1.8%, starbursts 0.9% — one control subject reported severe halos, none reported severe night glare or starbursts
- As a standard monofocal (non-diffractive, non-multifocal), this lens is not associated with the halo/glare/starburst profile seen in trifocal or EDOF-multifocal designs — the low rates above reflect general post-cataract-surgery background rates, not a lens-specific disturbance pattern
- General cataract-surgery risks apply: reactions to medicines, redness, scratchiness, light sensitivity, infection, bleeding, inflammation, tissue damage/swelling, increased eye pressure

**6. Contraindications / poor-candidate factors**
Not independently detailed in the available FDA record for this specific supplement. Does not correct pre-existing corneal astigmatism — patients with meaningful corneal astigmatism would need a toric version or glasses to correct residual astigmatism after surgery. Standard cataract-surgery candidacy considerations apply (eye health prior to surgery affects outcome).
