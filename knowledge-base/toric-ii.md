# TECNIS Toric II — Patient Labeling

**1. Metadata**
- Lens: TECNIS® Toric II — a shared astigmatism-correcting toric platform used across several J&J presbyopia-correcting and monofocal lens families in this knowledge base (e.g., TECNIS Synergy Toric II, TECNIS Eyhance Toric II, TECNIS Odyssey Toric II, TECNIS PureSee Toric II, TECNIS Symfony Toric II), each combining that lens's optical technology with the Toric II toric correction and haptic design
- Manufacturer: Johnson & Johnson Surgical Vision, Inc.
- Category: Shared toric (astigmatism-correcting) platform, generation "II" — a haptic/toric-design update from the earlier "Toric I" generation
- PMA: P980040, Supplement S103 (TECNIS Toric II 1-Piece IOLs, decided 2019-10-23) introduced the base Toric II 1-piece platform; each lens family's own supplement (documented individually elsewhere in this knowledge base) subsequently paired it with that lens's specific optic.
- **Source note:** No FDA SSED or patient brochure is publicly posted for the Toric II platform supplements. This document focuses specifically on rotational stability and astigmatism-correction performance — the toric-specific risk/benefit information that applies across all "Toric II" lens variants — sourced from peer-reviewed real-world and clinical studies, since no single FDA trial document covers the shared platform.

**2. Indications**
Used in patients who need both cataract removal and correction of pre-existing corneal astigmatism (typically ≥1.00D), in combination with whichever base lens technology is selected (monofocal, enhanced monofocal, EDOF, or multifocal — see that lens's own entry in this knowledge base for near/intermediate vision indications). Corrects astigmatism at the time of surgery; does not itself provide near or intermediate vision beyond what the paired base lens technology provides. Capsular bag placement.

**3. How it works**
A toric (cylindrical) surface correction added to the base lens's optic, aligned to the eye's steep corneal meridian to neutralize corneal astigmatism. "Toric II" refers to an updated haptic and rotational-stability design compared to the earlier "Toric I" generation used in older TECNIS toric lenses — intended to reduce the rate of postoperative lens rotation, which is the main toric-specific risk (a rotated toric lens loses some or all of its astigmatism correction and may need repositioning surgery).

**4. Expected visual outcomes**
Because "Toric II" is a shared platform, its distance/intermediate/near visual acuity outcomes depend on which base lens it's paired with (see that lens's own entry in this knowledge base). The data specific to the toric correction itself — the astigmatism-correction and rotational performance of the platform, tested independent of any specific base optic:
- **Chang et al. 2022, Clinical Ophthalmology** (post-market, prospective, multicenter, single-arm study; N=202 eyes/133 subjects, 7 US sites; base TECNIS Toric II platform, Models ZCU150–600, not paired with a specific presbyopia-correcting optic): at 3 months, mean uncorrected distance visual acuity (UDVA) was 20/20 (0.004±0.115 logMAR), and mean corrected distance visual acuity (CDVA) was 20/17 (−0.066±0.092 logMAR). 90.0% of eyes (180/200) achieved 20/25 or better UDVA, and 97.5% (195/200) were correctable to 20/25 or better. Mean cylinder (astigmatism) reduction was 91.9%, with 86.5% of eyes ending with 0.50D or less of residual astigmatism. Patient satisfaction with distance vision without glasses was 94.8% (73/77 bilaterally implanted patients); 89.5% (68/77) reported never needing distance correction after surgery. Surgeons were satisfied/very satisfied with uncorrected distance vision in 98.5% of eyes.
- **Toameh et al. 2025, Clinical Ophthalmology** (n=98 eyes, TECNIS Eyhance Toric II, prospective): mean absolute IOL rotation was 1.73°±1.64° from day 1 to week 1 (95% of eyes rotated ≤5°), and 1.13°±1.53° from week 1 to 3 months (98% of eyes rotated ≤5°) — well within the ANSI Z80.30 rotational-stability standard (>90% of eyes ≤5° axis change)
- **Hu 2023, Clinical Ophthalmology** (retrospective real-world chart review, N=993 eyes across 4 toric IOL types from a single surgeon): the rate of surgical repositioning due to clinically significant rotation was 0.6% for TECNIS Toric II (308 eyes) — notably lower than the earlier TECNIS Toric I generation (5.7% of 53 eyes) and comparable to or better than AcrySof Toric (2.2%) and enVista Toric (1.1%)

Population-level and real-world data — not a guarantee of any individual patient's outcome. This is observational/registry-style evidence, not a randomized controlled trial of the Toric II platform. Note: Chang et al. is J&J-sponsored (two authors are J&J employees, others disclose consulting relationships with J&J) — flagged for transparency, though the study design (independently-analyzed rotation measurements, prospective multicenter) is methodologically sound.

**5. Known side effects / visual disturbances** *(flagged for Martin's review — not auto-approved)*
Toric-specific risks, distinct from whichever base lens technology is paired with this platform (see that lens's own entry for its multifocal/EDOF-specific disturbance profile):
- If the lens rotates from its intended axis after surgery, astigmatism correction is reduced, and patients may notice visual distortion (e.g., objects appearing tilted) until the lens is repositioned or the rotation is otherwise addressed
- Per Hu 2023's real-world data, repositioning surgery was needed in 0.6% of Toric II eyes — a meaningfully low but non-zero rate
- General cataract-surgery risks (infection, bleeding, inflammation, increased eye pressure) apply, as with any IOL implantation

**6. Contraindications / poor-candidate factors**
Not independently established by FDA for this shared platform (no public SSED). By general toric-IOL logic and the exclusion criteria used in related trials in this knowledge base: patients with capsular or zonular instability (raising the risk of postoperative rotation), irregular astigmatism not well-characterized by a single toric correction, or astigmatism outside the available cylinder power range are generally poor candidates — treat as inference from the broader toric-IOL literature, not an FDA-confirmed Toric-II-specific finding.
